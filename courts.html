Volleyball Courts Around the World

Explore different types of volleyball courts and playing surfaces

Standard Indoor Volleyball Court

The standard indoor volleyball court is a rectangle measuring 18 meters (59 feet) long and 9 meters (29.5 feet) wide, divided into two 9m × 9m halves by a net. The net height is 2.43 meters (7 feet 11 5/8 inches) for men and 2.24 meters (7 feet 4 1/4 inches) for women.

The court is marked with boundary lines, attack lines 3 meters (9 feet 10 inches) from the center line, and service zones at the end of each court.

Beach Volleyball Court

Beach volleyball courts are smaller than indoor courts, measuring 16m × 8m (52.5 feet × 26.25 feet). The surface is sand, typically at least 40cm deep. The net height is the same as indoor volleyball.

Beach volleyball is played with teams of two players, with no fixed positions and no substitutions allowed. The rules are similar to indoor volleyball but with some key differences, such as no open-hand tips and a slightly different scoring system.

Other Court Variations

Snow Volleyball

Played on snow with teams of 3 players. The court dimensions are similar to beach volleyball.

Sitting Volleyball

Played by athletes with disabilities. The court is smaller (10m × 6m) and the net is lower (1.15m for men, 1.05m for women).

Grass Volleyball

Played on grass surfaces, often in recreational settings. Uses similar rules to beach volleyball.
